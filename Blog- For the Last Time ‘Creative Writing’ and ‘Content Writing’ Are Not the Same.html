For the Last Time: ‘Creative Writing’ and ‘Content Writing’ Are Not the Same

Content writing is often confused with creative writing; these two terms are used interchangeably. But those two functions are actually quite distinct — in their intent, the process involved, their goal, and most notably, the audience they target.

If you ask a non-writer about their difference, you may get a reply that creative writing and content writing are one and the same. But, now go ahead and ask the same question to a marketing writer, you’ll get a long-winded answer explaining how and why there’s a world of differences between those two concepts.

Now, let’s take a deeper dive into the world of creative writing vs. content writing and see how they differ from each other.

What Is Creative Writing?

Creative writing is defined as the writing process that falls outside the spectrum of business, professional, technical, and academic writing. Creative writing emphasizes more on the use of literal and figurative language, the aspects of character development, and other narrative crafts. Genres such as fiction, poetry, and creative non-fiction fall under the umbrella of creative writing.

What Is Content Writing?

Content writing is the process of creating content primarily for online consumption. It is created for a specific purpose like marketing, branding, and more. Content writing is used to attract potential clients and educate them about the products and services offered by a brand. Blogs, research articles, white papers, e-books, and infographics are some most commonly used content formats.

Mini-Lesson

Although it is critical to create a personalized connection with your target audience, developing profound imagery might distract and disengage them. So, if you are a writer with creative inclination, don’t make this rookie mistake while you work on the dark side aka content writing :)

So, What’s Your Calling?

In the battle between content writing vs creative writing, there is no clear winner. As a writer, you need to pick a side based on your skill set and preferences.

Are you one of those people with a flair for frivolous writing? Then creative writing might be your forte. If you can set aside your witty metaphors and focus on the purpose of the content then content writing is for you.

Although content writing and creative writing are two ends of a literary rope, both skills can be honed. All you need to do is understand the expectations, unlearn a few inherent practices, and switch gears accordingly. It takes time, patience, and a bit of practice, but it is possible!
